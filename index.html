Profile

Hello! I am Charlotte Bunnenberg, a researcher specializing in eco-evolutionary modelling. Throughout my career, I have recognized computer science as a powerful tool in the field of biology. To pursue this interdisciplinary interest, I began my education with a bachelor's degree in computer science and biology at the University of Osnabrück (Germany). Following that, I earned a master's degree in informatics for the natural sciences at Bielefeld University (Germany).
Currently, I am a PhD student in Greta Bocedi’s Research Group , working on the international project "The missing link: unraveling the role of genetic variation of beneficial arthropods in agro-ecosystems" funded by the Novo Nordisk Foundation.
My research focuses on exploring the influence of dynamic landscape processes on genetic diversity. I am particularly excited about exploring and developing ways to apply mathematical and computer science frameworks, such as graph theory, to in order to enhance landscape connectivity analysis.

Research

My research interests include:

  • Landscape connectivity analysis
  • Method development
  • Science communication
  • Data science
  • Theoretical population genetics
